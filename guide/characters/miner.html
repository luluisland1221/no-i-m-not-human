Character Overview

The Miner is an industrial worker who emerged from the depths of the earth, bringing with him the darkness and secrets of the underground world. His weathered appearance and knowledge of the deep places suggest either a human who has seen too much or something that came from below.

First Impression

Appears covered in dust and grime, carrying the weight of underground work

Key Behavior

Speaks of the earth and underground in reverent, sometimes unsettling terms

Earth Connection

Shows deep connection to the ground and what lies beneath

Background Story

The Deep Mining Operation

The Miner claims to have worked in one of the deepest mining operations beneath the city, digging for resources and minerals that most people don't know exist. He speaks of tunnels that go deeper than official records show, of chambers that predate human settlement, and of things that shouldn't exist beneath our feet.

The Discovery

His story changes when he speaks of what he and his crew discovered in the deepest shafts - not minerals or ores, but something alive and ancient. He describes finding veins of what looked like living rock, chambers that breathed, and whispers that came from the earth itself when drills broke through certain layers.

The Transformation

The Miner claims that prolonged exposure to these deep places changed him and his coworkers. They began to hear the earth's voice, to understand its rhythms, and to feel a connection to something vast and ancient that sleeps beneath the city. Some of them embraced this transformation, while others went mad or disappeared entirely.
