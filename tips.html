The Vibe Check Method

The "Politeness Test"

"She's polite. I'm letting her in. Thank you. Haha. Thanks so much. Seriously, now I get to live a little longer."

Genuine politeness and humility often indicate humans. Visitors tend to be either overly aggressive or suspiciously perfect.

Warning: Some visitors can fake politeness. Always verify with other tests.

Energy Management

"You won't have much energy during the day with this heat. So, choose who you want to test carefully. It's strenuous, draining work."

Conserve your testing energy for the most suspicious characters. Don't waste checks on obvious humans.

Strategy: Test newcomers first, then verify existing houseguests if resources allow.

Effective Testing Methods

The Name Test

Player: "What's your dad's name?"

Overly defensive or overly quick to identify your testing motives is suspicious.

The Background Question Test

Player: "What did you do before all this happened?"

Normal Response: "I was just chilling, watching flicks, hanging with the homies."

Suspicious Response: Vague, overly detailed, or story inconsistencies

Pressure Testing

"You got that damn attitude. Who you think you talking to like that on my front porch? It's my house."

Sometimes applying pressure reveals true nature. Humans get defensive but respectful; visitors may become threatening.

Empathy Assessment

"I'm just trying to keep it together, you know? So, you going to let me in?"

Genuine emotional responses and vulnerability often indicate humanity. Test their reactions to your own struggles.

The "Alone" Question

As mentioned in the game: "If anyone comes and asks if you're alone, say no and tell them there's eight bodybuilders in the back."

Monitor their reaction to this claim. Visitors often probe for isolation, while humans respect safety measures.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Should I trust people with perfect white teeth?

+

Based on player experience, perfect white teeth are the strongest indicator of being a visitor. However, as one player noted: "You know it's possible to have good teeth in general. It don't mean she's 100% a visitor." Use this as a strong warning sign, but consider other factors too.

How do I know if someone is lying about their background?

+

Look for inconsistencies in their stories, overly detailed or rehearsed-sounding responses, and defensive reactions when questioned. Real humans tend to have more natural, sometimes imperfect memories of their past.

What should I do if I'm unsure about someone?

+

Trust your gut feeling. As experienced players advise: "If you got bad vibes, you ain't coming in the crib." It's better to be safe than sorry. You can also delay the decision by engaging them in more conversation to gather more information.

How do I handle FEMA encounters?

+

Be cooperative but cautious. FEMA employees should have proper identification and equipment. However, as shown in gameplay: "We'll just take one person and be on our way" - even legitimate authorities may pose risks. Never volunteer information about being alone.

What's the most reliable identification method?

+

Based on real gameplay analysis, a combination of methods works best: 1) Physical characteristics (especially teeth), 2) Behavioral analysis, 3) Dialogue testing, and 4) Your gut feeling. No single method is foolproof.

Should I feel bad about rejecting people who seem genuinely in need?

+

Remember the stakes: you're protecting yourself from creatures that can "imitate the appearance and behavior of humans." As one player learned the hard way, being too sympathetic can be fatal. Trust your survival instincts.

House Management Strategy

Window Observation Technique

"TV's great and all, but there's no better source of info than your own two eyes." Regularly check windows for external threats and visitor behavior patterns.

The Phone Contact Method

Maintain contacts with trusted neighbors. As shown: "We'll keep in touch on the phone" - communication networks can provide vital information and backup.

Resource Conservation

"You won't have much energy during the day with this heat." Plan your testing schedule around energy availability and prioritize high-risk individuals.
