About No, I'm not a Human

No, I'm not a Human is a psychological horror game developed by Trioskaz and published by Critical Reflex. Released on September 15, 2025, this first-person horror visual novel challenges players' perceptions of trust and survival through atmospheric storytelling and innovative identification mechanics.

The Apocalyptic Setting

Set in a near-future world devastated by catastrophic solar activity, daylight has become deadly and survivors live nocturnally. The sun's radiation forces humanity indoors during day, emerging only at night to scavenge and survive. This inverted existence has created a society operating in reverse, with profound psychological toll on survivors navigating this collapsed world.

The Visitor Threat

Mysterious entities called "Visitors" have emerged from the planet's depths, possessing the ability to mimic human appearance with frightening accuracy. Their integration into human society represents a biological invasion that preys on vulnerable survivors. Visitors understand human social structures and exploit the need for community and shelter, creating psychological warfare that questions the foundations of trust and human connection.

Identification Methods and Challenges

Government broadcasts provide daily identification criteria for detecting Visitors: abnormal dental characteristics, ocular abnormalities, absence of body hair, behavioral irregularities, and physiological inconsistencies. However, identification is complicated by incomplete trait expression and humans exhibiting similar characteristics due to stress or radiation exposure, creating persistent uncertainty.

Gameplay Mechanics and Strategic Depth

Players assume the role of a lone survivor in a peripheral house, dealing with nightly visitors seeking shelter. Each arrival presents life-or-death decisions requiring careful energy resource management. Players must balance thorough examinations against energy depletion, prioritizing which visitors warrant closer inspection based on initial observations and behavioral analysis.

Moral Complexity and Narrative Branching

Unlike typical horror games, this title explores nuanced moral decision-making under extreme pressure. Players navigate complex ethical landscapes where survival instincts conflict with humanitarian values, with each decision carrying narrative weight. The branching story structure features multiple endings reflecting different approaches to the survival crisis, encouraging repeated playthroughs to explore all possible outcomes.

Atmospheric Design and Psychological Horror

Visual and audio design create constant tension and unease through the contrast between nighttime safety and terrifying daylight. Environmental storytelling through window views of the collapsing world adds contextual depth to survival challenges. Psychological horror elements emerge from unsettling Visitor mimicry and the protagonist's mental erosion under constant life-or-death pressure, exploring themes of trust, identity, and human connection.
